# Calprotectin Stool Test: A Key Marker for Diagnosing IBD and Monitoring Gut Health

## Introduction

A calprotectin stool test measures fecal calprotectin, a protein released by white blood cells during intestinal inflammation. Because it is stable in stool and correlates with neutrophil activity in the gut, fecal calprotectin is widely used as a non-invasive marker to screen for and monitor inflammatory bowel disease (IBD).

## What is calprotectin and why it matters

Calprotectin is a heterodimeric protein abundant in neutrophils. When the intestinal mucosa is inflamed, neutrophils migrate into the gut lumen and release calprotectin, which can then be detected in stool samples. Elevated fecal calprotectin levels are associated with active inflammation and help distinguish inflammatory disorders from non-inflammatory functional conditions such as irritable bowel syndrome (IBS).

## How the calprotectin stool test works

The test requires a small stool sample, typically collected at home using a kit and returned to a laboratory for quantitative analysis. Results are reported as concentration (µg/g of stool). Common interpretive thresholds vary slightly by laboratory, but low values generally suggest the absence of significant intestinal inflammation, while higher values indicate active inflammation that may warrant further investigation.

## Role in diagnosing and managing IBD

The calprotectin stool test is frequently employed when clinicians suspect IBD, including Crohn’s disease and ulcerative colitis. Elevated fecal calprotectin supports the likelihood of inflammatory pathology and can reduce unnecessary invasive procedures when values are low. However, it is not disease-specific: infections, certain medications, and colorectal cancer can also raise calprotectin, so results must be interpreted in clinical context.

For Crohn’s disease, fecal calprotectin is useful both at initial assessment and for ongoing monitoring. Serial measurements can indicate treatment response or predict relapse before clinical symptoms worsen. This objective measure assists clinicians in tailoring therapy intensity and scheduling endoscopic assessment when needed.

## Benefits of early detection and monitoring

Early identification of intestinal inflammation enables timely treatment adjustments, which can reduce complications and improve long-term outcomes. Routine monitoring with fecal calprotectin helps track disease activity, guide escalation or de-escalation of therapy, and prioritize patients for endoscopy when noninvasive markers indicate active inflammation.
