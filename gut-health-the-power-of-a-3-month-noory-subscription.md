In the fast-paced, often confusing world of health and wellness, finding clarity is difficult. What is InnerBuddies and who is Noory?

InnerBuddies is a health platform that translates gut microbiome analysis into individualized dietary guidance. The gut microbiome has been associated with digestion, metabolic regulation, immunity, and aspects of mental health in peer-reviewed studies. By mapping microbial composition, a personalized plan can target specific imbalances or support microbial diversity rather than relying on one-size-fits-all recommendations.

Noory is the digital nutrition coach within the InnerBuddies ecosystem. Combining behavioral design, nutritional science, and microbiome-informed insights, Noory delivers recipes and meal plans adapted to a user’s biological profile and lifestyle constraints. The approach is data-driven and intended to be practical: it prioritizes feasible substitutions, culturally appropriate foods, and progressive changes that can be sustained.

What the 3-month program includes

1) Microbiome-based assessment: The program begins with a home collection kit and laboratory sequencing to characterize key taxa and functional markers. The resulting report highlights microbial strengths and imbalances, digestive and metabolic indicators, and potential signals related to inflammation or gut–brain interactions. This foundational data informs subsequent recommendations. <a href='https://innerbuddies-gut-microbiome.github.io/innerbuddies/transforming-health-with-the-eu-nutriome-project'>EU Nutriome Project</a>

2) Personalized nutrition plan: Based on the microbiome profile, Noory generates adaptive meal plans that emphasize foods likely to support microbial diversity and metabolic health, and de-emphasize foods that may provoke imbalance. Plans include portion guidance and optional supplement suggestions when supported by the data and clinical rationale.

3) Respect for context and sustainability: Noory’s recommendations account for dietary preferences (vegan, vegetarian, pescatarian, flexitarian), cultural practices, and ethical choices to enhance adherence. Personalization increases the likelihood that changes will be maintained, addressing a common limitation of generic diets.

Evidence-based, gradual change

The emphasis is on structural, incremental shifts—small, biologically rational modifications that accumulate over months. This aligns with behavioral medicine principles showing that gradual habit formation and context-sensitive interventions produce more durable outcomes than rapid, rigid regimens.
