# Saccharomyces boulardii: The Unique Yeast That Fights Diarrhea & Restores Balance

Gut health is increasingly recognized as central to overall wellbeing. One well-studied microbe in this space is [Saccharomyces boulardii](https://www.innerbuddies.com/blogs/gut-health/saccharomyces-boulardii-the-probiotic-yeast-that-fights-diarrhea-restores-microbiome-balance), a probiotic yeast with distinct properties compared with bacterial probiotics. This article reviews what it is, how it works, evidence for common uses, and practical safety considerations.

## What is Saccharomyces boulardii?

Saccharomyces boulardii is a yeast first identified in the 1920s. Because it is a yeast rather than a bacterium, it tolerates environmental and pharmacological stressors that can reduce bacterial probiotic viability. Its resilience means it can persist in the intestinal environment during antibiotic exposure and contribute to restoring microbial balance when bacterial populations are depleted.

## Mechanisms of action

The beneficial effects of S. boulardii are mediated through multiple, complementary mechanisms. It can:

- Support intestinal barrier integrity and reduce inflammatory signaling.
- Interfere with pathogen adhesion and neutralize toxins produced by some harmful bacteria.
- Modulate host immune responses in the gut mucosa.
- Promote recolonization dynamics by creating a more favourable environment for beneficial microbes.

These mechanisms are consistent with clinical findings where S. boulardii reduces the duration and severity of certain diarrheal illnesses, particularly antibiotic-associated diarrhea.

## Evidence and common uses

Randomized trials and meta-analyses have examined S. boulardii for conditions such as antibiotic-associated diarrhea and acute infectious diarrhea. Results generally indicate a reduction in incidence or duration of diarrhea compared with placebo in many, but not all, study settings. Effect sizes vary by population, strain, dose, and study quality, so context matters when interpreting results.

Typical study dosages reported in literature range from about 250 mg to 1 g per day, depending on the formulation and indication. Safety profiles are favourable in immunocompetent individuals, with transient gastrointestinal symptoms (e.g., gas, bloating) reported occasionally.

## Safety considerations

Because S. boulardii is a live yeast, it is contraindicated or used with caution in people with severe immunosuppression, central venous catheters, or some critical illnesses due to rare reports of fungemia. Patients with these risk factors should consult a clinician before starting any live microbial supplement. For most healthy adults, adverse effects are uncommon and mild.

## Summary

Saccharomyces boulardii is a distinctive probiotic yeast with mechanisms that support intestinal barrier function and reduce diarrheal symptoms in many studies. It is generally well tolerated in healthy individuals but requires caution in immunocompromised patients.
