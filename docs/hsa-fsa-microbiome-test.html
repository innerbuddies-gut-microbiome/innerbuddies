With gut health increasingly recognized as a factor in digestion, immunity and mood, many people consider microbiome testing to inform diagnosis or treatment. A common question is whether these tests can be paid for with pre-tax funds from a Health Savings Account (HSA) or Flexible Spending Account (FSA). This article explains how eligibility is determined, what documentation helps, and where to look for authoritative guidance.

When Is a Microbiome Test an HSA-Eligible Expense?

HSAs reimburse qualified medical expenses as defined by the IRSâ€”costs incurred to diagnose, treat, mitigate or prevent disease. A microbiome test may meet that standard when it is used in a diagnostic context: for example, to investigate chronic gastrointestinal symptoms (bloating, abdominal pain), suspected SIBO, inflammatory bowel disease, or other conditions where a clinician expects results to inform care. If a licensed provider orders or recommends the test and documents the medical necessity, reimbursement likelihood increases.

Useful documentation includes an itemized invoice, a Letter of Medical Necessity (LMN) from a provider outlining symptoms and rationale, and any lab reports showing clinical-grade methods. Labs that operate under CLIA standards and follow FDA-compliant workflows strengthen the clinical credibility of results.

FSA Coverage for At-Home and Lab-Based Tests

FSAs follow similar IRS guidelines. Because FSAs are employer-sponsored and often have strict documentation requirements, the same rules apply: tests intended for diagnosis or monitoring of a medical condition are more likely to be eligible than tests purchased purely for general wellness or curiosity. FSA administrators commonly request a receipt, LMN, and sometimes an Explanation of Benefits (EOB) if insurance was involved first.

For at-home kits, confirm that the processing lab meets clinical standards and that your provider can reasonably justify the test as medically necessary. If so, submit well-organized documentation to your FSA administrator for review.

Practical Steps to Improve Reimbursement Odds

  • Obtain a Letter of Medical Necessity from a licensed clinician describing symptoms and how the test will guide treatment.
  • Keep itemized receipts and lab paperwork showing CLIA certification or clinical-grade methods.
  • Ask your HSA/FSA administrator about pre-approval or claim submission procedures.
