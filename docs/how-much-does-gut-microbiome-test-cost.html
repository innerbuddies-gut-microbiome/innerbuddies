The gut microbiome test has become an increasingly common diagnostic and wellness tool. Costs vary widely depending on the sequencing method, the breadth of biomarkers included, and whether clinical interpretation is provided. This article summarizes typical price ranges and the factors that drive those differences so you can make a more informed decision.

Typical price ranges and what they cover

Basic consumer-focused microbiome tests that use 16S rRNA sequencing generally cost between $89 and $150. These provide genus-level bacterial profiles and basic diversity metrics. Tests using whole genome shotgun (WGS) sequencing—able to resolve species and some strain-level signals and detect non-bacterial organisms—tend to run from about $200 to $400.

Comprehensive gut-health panels that include inflammation markers (for example, calprotectin), intestinal permeability indicators, short-chain fatty acid analysis, or tests for specific pathogens can cost $400–$800 or more when bundled with professional interpretation. When assessing the total price, note whether the kit price includes shipping, lab processing, a digital report, and any follow-up consultation.

What influences the cost?

Major cost drivers include the sequencing technology, laboratory accreditation, bioinformatics pipelines, and whether results are delivered with actionable context. Laboratories that perform validated clinical assays and provide clinician-facing reports usually charge more than direct-to-consumer services designed for wellness insights.

Operational factors such as sample logistics, turnaround time, and customer support also add to fees. Some providers offer subscription models or modular add-ons (e.g., dietary recommendations or sample re-analysis), which may change the effective cost over time.

Choosing a test based on needs

If your goal is a broad wellness snapshot, a 16S-based test can be a cost-effective starting point. For clinical questions—recurrent gastrointestinal symptoms, suspected infections, or diagnostic confirmation—selecting a testing option with higher resolution and clinician interpretation is generally more appropriate.

Practical considerations

Confirm what is and isn’t included in the stated price: kit delivery and return shipping, lab processing, depth of sequencing, and the format of the report. Also check whether tests are eligible for HSA/FSA reimbursement when ordered through a clinician.
