[How to reset my gut?](https://www.innerbuddies.com/blogs/gut-health/how-to-reset-my-gut)

A gut reset is a structured approach to restore microbial balance, improve digestion, and support intestinal barrier function. The process combines personalized assessment, dietary change, targeted supplementation, and lifestyle adjustments. Evidence from microbiome research supports tailored strategies because individual microbial communities respond differently to interventions.

Why test first

Microbiome testing provides a snapshot of bacterial diversity, relative abundances, and functional markers (for example, short-chain fatty acid production). Interpreting these results helps prioritize interventions—whether to increase fiber types, introduce specific probiotic strains, or focus on detoxification pathways. Core, evidence-based steps

1. Personalize the diet: Emphasize a variety of plant fibers (aiming for many distinct plant foods weekly), whole grains, legumes, and polyphenol-rich items (berries, green tea, olive oil). Different fibers feed different microbial niches; diversity is linked to resilience.

2. Reintroduce fermented foods and measured probiotics: Foods such as yogurt, kefir, sauerkraut, and kimchi can introduce live microbes and beneficial metabolites. Use targeted probiotic strains if testing indicates specific deficiencies (for example, certain Lactobacillus or Bifidobacterium species). A product reference for testing can be helpful for interpretation: <a href='https://www.innerbuddies.com/products/microbiome-test'>InnerBuddies microbiome test</a>.

3. Support gut repair and digestion: Digestive enzymes, glutamine, and collagen or bone-broth–derived nutrients may support mucosal repair. Focus on adequate protein, omega-3 fats, and micronutrients that support barrier integrity.

4. Address detoxification safely: Increase soluble and insoluble fiber to bind and remove metabolites, maintain hydration, and consider liver-supportive herbs under clinical supervision. Monitoring symptoms and test markers can flag when detox pathways are overloaded.

5. Lifestyle factors: Regular physical activity, sufficient sleep, and stress reduction (mindfulness, breathing exercises) are consistently associated with improved microbiome composition and reduced inflammation.

Measure and iterate

Improvements often appear within 4–12 weeks, but some shifts require longer-term maintenance. Repeat microbiome testing at defined intervals to evaluate changes in diversity and function and to adjust interventions scientifically rather than by trial alone.

Practical considerations

Avoid unnecessary antibiotics and ultra-processed foods that reduce fiber and introduce additives linked to dysbiosis. Track symptoms and dietary changes to correlate interventions with outcomes. If complex conditions (SIBO, persistent inflammation) are suspected, coordinate testing and treatment with a qualified clinician.

Summary

A gut reset is most effective when guided by data and implemented holistically: increase dietary diversity, support beneficial microbes, clear disruptive influences, repair the gut lining, and address lifestyle contributors. Iterative testing and measured interventions promote durable microbial balance and improved digestive health.
