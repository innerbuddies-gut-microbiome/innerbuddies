## How do I reset my gut?

A gut reset is a structured approach to restore microbial balance and digestive function using data-driven interventions. Rather than short-term detoxes, an evidence-based reset targets root causes—microbial imbalance (dysbiosis), inflammation, and impaired gut barrier function—so the system can recover and maintain resilience.

Microbiome testing provides actionable insight into which microbes are present, which are depleted, and which metabolic pathways may be disrupted. For a concise, practical primer on personalized gut resets, see the full guide here: [How do I reset my gut?](https://www.innerbuddies.com/blogs/gut-health/how-do-i-reset-my-gut).

### Why test first

Sequencing-based stool analysis identifies diversity metrics, dominant taxa, potential pathogens or overgrowths, and functional signals (for example, short-chain fatty acid production). These results allow clinicians and informed individuals to choose targeted dietary changes, specific prebiotics or probiotics, and supportive nutrients—rather than relying on one-size-fits-all regimens.

### Core phases of a practical reset

1. Remove: Reduce or eliminate dietary triggers (highly processed foods, excess added sugars, unnecessary antibiotics, alcohol) and address identified overgrowths with appropriate antimicrobial approaches when indicated. 
2. Replenish: Restore nutrients and digestive support—digestive enzymes, zinc, and amino acids can be helpful when deficiency or malabsorption is suspected.
3. Repair: Support mucosal healing with nutrients such as L‑glutamine, collagen peptides, and micronutrients that support epithelial integrity.
4. Reinoculate: Reintroduce beneficial microbes through diverse, fiber-rich foods, fermented foods, and targeted probiotic strains based on test findings.

A data-guided plan will typically progress over 30–90 days for measurable shifts; retesting at 3–6 month intervals helps confirm progress and refine interventions. ### Supporting habits for durable change

Habit-level factors strongly influence microbiome stability: dietary plant diversity (aim for many different plant foods weekly), regular physical activity, predictable sleep, and stress management. Polyphenol-rich foods, prebiotic fibers, and limiting unnecessary antimicrobial exposures support diversity and functional resilience.

### Practical precautions

Avoid extreme cleanses or prolonged fasting without clinical oversight. Use binders or antimicrobials only when indicated by clinical assessment or test results. Work with a qualified practitioner if you have complex symptoms, immune suppression, or are taking multiple medications.

In summary, a gut reset based on microbiome data combines targeted removal of stressors, nutrient and mucosal support, and tailored reinoculation to rebuild a balanced ecosystem—supported by ongoing monitoring and sustainable lifestyle practices.
