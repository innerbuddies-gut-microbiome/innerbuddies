## Understanding the microbiome and testing

The microbiome is a diverse ecosystem of bacteria, archaea, viruses, and fungi that interact with human physiology. When balanced, these microbes assist in fiber fermentation, vitamin production, and immune regulation; when imbalanced, they can contribute to bloating, inflammation, and metabolic changes.

Microbiome analysis can be performed with stool-based sequencing or biomarker tests. For actionable dietary guidance, sequencing that characterizes bacterial composition is commonly used, as is measurement of inflammation-related markers like zonulin and calprotectin for specific clinical contexts (<a href='https://innerbuddies-gut-microbiome.github.io/innerbuddies/zonulin-stool-test-calprotectin-explained-innerbuddies-gut-health'>zonulin and calprotectin testing</a>). Detailed sequencing approaches, such as full-length 16S rRNA methods, can provide higher-resolution taxonomic data that inform personalized recommendations (<a href='https://innerbuddies-gut-microbiome.github.io/innerbuddies/full-length-16s-rrna-sequencing'>full-length 16S rRNA sequencing</a>).

## What the InnerBuddies diet plan emphasizes

The InnerBuddies diet plan focuses on tailoring nutrition to measured microbiome features rather than applying a uniform diet. Key nutritional components include:

- Dietary fiber diversity: Different fibers feed different bacteria. Beans, whole grains, fruits, and vegetables support fiber-fermenting taxa that produce beneficial short-chain fatty acids.
- Prebiotics: Specific compounds like inulin (found in garlic, onions, asparagus) selectively nourish beneficial microbes.
- Fermented foods and probiotics: Foods such as yogurt or kefir can introduce or support live microbes, although effects are strain-specific and vary by individual.
- Targeted reductions: Where lab results indicate an overabundance of taxa associated with inflammation or dysbiosis, moderation of certain foods (for example, highly processed sugars or excessive red meat) can be advised.

By combining sequencing data with dietary patterns, the plan seeks to increase beneficial taxa and reduce drivers of dysbiosis with tailored food choices.
